Our Type 2 Diabetes program uses portions of the Health Belief Model. The program includes a detailed description of the condition, though its primary focus is on self-management and self-monitoring. This program includes condition information around fasting glucose, hyperglycemia, hypoglycemia, glucose monitoring, and how carbs affect glucose levels. We also include information on annual exams, daily checks (i.e. eye, feet and dental checks), the importance of healthy lifestyle changes, and risk factors associated with smoking.

The CareMessage Hypertension (high blood pressure) program adapts the Health Belief Model, which places value on condition knowledge, perceived severity, and self efficacy. This program breaks down condition information around systolic and diastolic blood pressure, the two types of readings for hypertension. We created this program for people who have been recently diagnosed with high blood pressure, or any individual who is interested in making lifestyle changes to normalize their blood pressure levels.

Programs handcrafted for your patient.

Patient Attitude

Address cultural barriers to accepting a diagnosis and help patients self-actualize and understand factors that motivate them to change their behaviors.

Medical Terms, Simplified

Enable patients to understand difficult medical concepts, such as the relationship between glucose and insulin.

Test Results

Help patients understand the significance of their test results, such as HbA1C and cholesterol, and remind them when they're due for their next checkup.

Self Management

Teach patients to self-manage various aspects of their condition, such as conducting regular foot exams at home.

Remote Monitoring

Remind patients to measure blood glucose and other metrics regularly, and allow them to text and store these numbers in a secure database.

Flexible Engagement

Our programs vary in length from 12 to 25 weeks. Patients can automatically be enrolled in follow up programs based on their preferences.
