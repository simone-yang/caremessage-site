Type 2 Diabetes

  • EN
  • SP
  • True or False? When someone has diabetes, they cannot eat anything with sugar. Text TRUE or FALSE
  • TRUE
  • It's actually false! All foods can fit into a healthy meal plan. You want to eat small meals throughout the day to keep your sugar levels at a healthy point.
  • How many people do you know that have diabetes? A)None B)1-5 C)5-10 D)More than 10 (Text the letter)
  • A
  • Sometimes it can be hard for other people to say they have diabetes. Talk to your doctor about the diabetes support groups at the clinic.
  • What glucose number should you have in the morning before eating breakfast? A)Less than 90 B)Between 90-130 C)Above 130
  • B
  • Right! You can talk to your doctor about what number is right for you. In general, a number between 90-130 is normal.
  • Verdadero o falso? Cuando uno tiene diabetes, no puede comer nada con azucar. Responda VERDAD o FALSO
  • Verdad
  • La respuesta es falso! Usted puede comer de todo. Coma pequenas cantidades durante el dia para mantener su nivel de azucar saludable.
  • Cuantas personas conoce que tienen diabetes? A)Ninguna B)1-5 C)5-10 D)Mas de 10 (Responda con la letra)
  • A
  • Aveces es dificil para muchas personas decir que tienen diabetes. Hable con su doctor sobre los grupos de apoyo de la diabetes en la clinica.
  • En que numero cree que debe de estar su azucar en ayunas? A)Menos de 90 B)De 90 a 130 C)Mas de 130
  • B
  • Si. Hable con su doctor sobre que numero debe de tener. Se recomienda que su azucar este entre 90-130 al despertar en ayunas.

SugarTalk and PlaticaDulce are year-long programs that comprise shorter 4-6 week long modules addressing topics most relevant to type 2 diabetics, such as properly injecting insulin, recognizing high or low blood glucose or avoiding culturally-specific foods that tend to increase blood glucose. The programs are tailored to various factors, including disease progression, age, gender, ethnicity, and cultural preferences.

Hypertension

  • B
  • Feeling confused is normal. High blood pressure is a health condition that you can control with some lifestyle changes. We are here to help!
  • Do you think that everyone who has high blood pressure knows they have it? Text YES or NO
  • Yes
  • High blood pressure is sometimes called the silent killer. Many people do not know their pressure is high because they do not feel sick from it.
  • Which of these is ok to have right before your next doctor's appointment? A)Coke B)Food C)Water

  • Yes! Don't drink caffeine or alcohol and don't eat 30 min before your doctor's appointment. It can give the wrong blood pressure readings.

Heart2Heart and DeCorazón are year-long programs that, like our diabetes programs, cover topics most relevant to hypertensives, such as checking blood pressure, reducing sodium intake, increasing exercise, or properly taking medication. The programs are meant to help support and motivate patients as they make lifestyle changes.

Programs handcrafted for your patient.

Patient Attitude

Address cultural barriers to accepting a diagnosis and help patients self-actualize and understand factors that motivate them to change their behaviors.

Medical Terms, Simplified

Enable patients to understand difficult medical concepts, such as the relationship between glucose and insulin.

Test Results

Help patients understand the significance of their test results, such as HbA1C and cholesterol, and remind them when they're due for their next checkup.

Self Management

Teach patients to self-manage various aspects of their condition, such as conducting regular foot exams at home.

Remote Monitoring

Remind patients to measure blood glucose and other metrics regularly, and allow them to text and store these numbers in a secure database.

Flexible Engagement

Patients can be enrolled in the entire year-long program or any of the shorter 4-6-week-long modules.
